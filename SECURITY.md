# Security Policy

## Our Commitment

At BGP in the Cloud (BIC), we take the security of our software seriously. We are committed to protecting our users and their data. We believe that a strong security posture is critical to building trust and ensuring the long-term success of our product.

We appreciate the valuable role that independent security researchers play in the ecosystem, and we encourage the responsible disclosure of any security vulnerabilities you may find.

## Reporting a Vulnerability

If you discover a security vulnerability in this project, please report it to us privately.

**Please do not report security vulnerabilities through public GitHub issues.**

Instead, please send an email to:

**jparrish@jpps.us**

When reporting a vulnerability, please include the following details:

- A clear and concise description of the vulnerability.
- The steps required to reproduce the issue.
- The version of the software where the vulnerability was discovered.
- Any proof-of-concept code, screenshots, or logs that could help us understand the issue.

## Our Process

Once we receive a vulnerability report, we will:

1.  Acknowledge receipt of the report within 48 hours.
2.  Investigate the issue and confirm the vulnerability.
3.  Work on a patch to address the vulnerability.
4.  Release the patch as part of our next regular update cycle. We will not disclose the vulnerability publicly until a patch is available.
5.  Credit the reporting researcher in the release notes, unless they prefer to remain anonymous.

We thank you for your help in keeping BGP in the Cloud (BIC) secure.
